0229: 3 Methods
0230: 3.1 Overview
0231: We proposeFantasyVLN, a VLN framework that integrates multimodal reasoning modes as its
0232: core design, while enabling implicit reasoning for efficient inference. As shown in Figure 2,
0233: FantasyVLNinternalizes diverse CoT reasoning patterns across modalities through end-to-end
0234: joint training, and enhances the non-CoT reasoning mode via a cross-mode alignment constraint.
0235: This enables combining the advantages of both textual and visual CoT reasoning without incurring
0236: explicit CoT reasoning time. Moreover, we perform visual CoT reasoning in the latent space of the
0237: V AR model (Tian et al., 2024), which improves training and inference efficiency compared with
0238: pixel-space methods.
0239: Below, we introduce the problem setup, cross-mode alignment constraint, unified multimodal implicit
0240: reasoning and latent visual CoT learning.
0241: 3.2 Problem Setup
0242: VLN aims to develop an embodied agentπθ that navigates continuous 3D environmentsObased on a
0243: natural language instructionIand visual observations, which can be formulated as a non-Markovian
0244: temporal decision problem. Lets 0 denote the initial state, i.e., location and orientation, andUdenote
0245: the action space. At each timestept, the agentπ θ receives multi-view visual observationso t ∈ O
0246: and predicts future actionsA t ∈ Uconditioned on the instructionIand historical observations
0247: {o≤t}. Subsequently, the predicted actionsA t are executed, transferring the agentπ θ to a new state
0248: according to the environment dynamics. This interaction process continues until a stop action is
0249: executed or the maximum stepTis reached.

0282: 3.4 Unified Multimodal Chain-of-Thought
0283: Building on CompV-CoT, we further present aUnified Multimodal Chain-of-Thought(UM-CoT)
0284: framework that integrates textual, compressed visual, and multimodal reasoning within a single
0285: agent.
0286: Textual CoT in VLN.Textual CoT (T-CoT) models the agent’s reasoning as an explicit semantic
0287: planning process that bridges language understanding and action decision. Instead of directly
0288: mapping instructions to actions, the agent first generates textual intermediate reasoning steps bTt.
0289: These reasoning steps then provide structured causal guidance for predicting subsequent actions
0290: bAt, enabling interpretable and more reliable decision-making. Specifically, the intermediate steps
0291: typically involve inferring subgoals from the instruction, assessing progress through current and
0292: historical visual observations, and identifying actionable cues for achieving the next objective.
0293: CompV-CoT as Visual CoT.For visual CoT (V-CoT), we directly adopt the CompV-CoT intro-
0294: duced in Sec. 3.3 as the visual reasoning mode in UM-CoT. In this setting, the agent imagines
0295: future observations in the V AR latent space by predicting compressed visual tokens, and then infers
0296: actions conditioned on the imagined latent trajectory. Compared with pixel-space prediction, this
0297: CompV-CoT design yields more efficient and stable visual reasoning.
0298: Multimodal CoT in VLN.Multimodal CoT (MM-CoT) is defined as a native combination of T-
0299: CoT and CompV-CoT, where the agent is required to generate paired textual–visual reasoning steps.
0300: We denote the multimodal reasoning trace asM t = [T t,V t], which jointly encodes semantic plans
0301: and imagined future observations, and use it to guide subsequent action prediction.

0584: 4.4 More Results
0585: Training Efficiency.As shown in Table 2, existing visual CoT methods (e.g., WorldVLA) achieve
0586: limited navigation accuracy on the LH-VLN benchmark, failing to generalize to long-horizon sce-
0587: narios. To understand the underlying cause, we compare their training efficiency with our unified
0588: multimodal formulation. As shown in Figure 5, WorldVLA exhibits slow and unstable convergence,
0589: requiring over10k iterations to reach moderate token prediction accuracy. This indicates that pixel-
0590: level V-CoT learning delivers weak gradient signals, as the model must reconstruct high-dimensional
0591: visual tokens for each reasoning step. In contrast,FantasyVLNconverges rapidly within a few
0592: thousand iterations, reflecting stable supervision and more efficient learning dynamics. This im-
0593: provement stems from our CompV-CoT design, where visual reasoning operates in a compact latent
0594: space encoded by the pretrained V AR compressor. By replacing dense pixel reconstruction with com-
0595: pressed latent prediction, the model learns richer multimodal reasoning cues under a substantially
0596: lighter optimization burden. Overall, these results highlight that CompV-CoT not only enhances
0597: reasoning efficiency but also yields more stable and interpretable learning behavior, contributing to
0598: the superior navigation accuracy ofFantasyVLNin long-horizon tasks.
0599: Explicit vs. Implicit Reasoning.FantasyVLNsupports both explicit and implicit reasoning,
0600: enabling us to systematically compare their effectiveness across different CoT modalities. As sum-
0601: marized in Table 6, we evaluate the T-CoT, V-CoT, and MM-CoT models under two inference modes.
0602: For each case, the model is jointly trained with its corresponding CoT mode and the direct prediction
0603: pathway. During inference, using the CoT branch corresponds to explicit reasoning, while employing
0604: the direct pathway corresponds to implicit reasoning. Overall, implicit reasoning consistently yields
0605: higher navigation accuracy, particularly under multimodal settings. In MM-CoT, implicit inference
0606: achieves the best performance with2.44SR and11.01ISR, surpassing the explicit counterpart by a
0607: large margin. This result aligns with the observations in Aux-Think (Wang et al., 2025), suggesting
0608: that explicit CoT decoding may amplify cumulative reasoning errors across long trajectories. We
0609: attribute this phenomenon to two key factors: (i) the limited training data of LH-VLN (only18k
0610: trajectory slices of five steps each) makes explicit CoT sequences prone to overfitting and error prop-
0611: agation; (ii) explicit reasoning expands temporal dependencies, causing misaligned textual or visual
0612: CoT tokens to accumulate deviations over time. In contrast, implicit reasoning benefits from cross-
0613: mode alignment during training, allowing the model to internalize reasoning cues while maintaining
0614: stable and efficient inference.
